# Learning Strategies

**Purpose**: How to study effectively based on your goal and timeline.

---

## Study Approaches by Goal

### Goal 1: Interview Preparation (2-3 months)

**Timeline**: 8-12 weeks

**Week 1-2**: Foundations
- Study thinking toolkit (all modules)
- Study answer frameworks
- Practice problem framing

**Week 3-4**: Role-specific knowledge
- Study your role path content
- Understand key concepts
- Review reference materials

**Week 5-6**: Scenario practice
- Practice system design scenarios
- Practice behavioral questions
- Time yourself

**Week 7-8**: Interview preparation
- Mock interviews
- Review checklist
- Final preparation

**Week 9+**: Final review
- Review weak areas
- Practice common questions
- Prepare questions to ask

---

### Goal 2: Skill Development (6+ months)

**Timeline**: 6-12 months

**Month 1-2**: Foundations
- Deep dive into thinking toolkit
- Practice regularly
- Build habits

**Month 3-4**: Role-specific knowledge
- Study role path in depth
- Work through all scenarios
- Build understanding

**Month 5-6**: Advanced topics
- Study advanced patterns
- Practice complex scenarios
- Build portfolio

**Month 7+**: Continuous improvement
- Regular practice
- Stay updated
- Mentor others

---

### Goal 3: Quick Review (1-2 weeks)

**Timeline**: 1-2 weeks

**Day 1-2**: Quick foundations
- Review thinking toolkit (skim)
- Review answer frameworks
- Understand structure

**Day 3-5**: Role-specific review
- Review role path README
- Study key concepts
- Practice 2-3 scenarios

**Day 6-7**: Interview prep
- Review interview checklist
- Practice common questions
- Prepare questions to ask

---

## Time Management Tips

### Daily Practice (30-60 minutes)
- 15 min: Study one concept
- 15 min: Practice one scenario
- 15 min: Review and reflect
- 15 min: Note-taking

### Weekly Practice (3-5 hours)
- 2 hours: Study new content
- 2 hours: Practice scenarios
- 1 hour: Review and reflect

### Before Interview (1 week)
- 2 hours/day: Review and practice
- Focus on weak areas
- Practice mock interviews

---

## Practice Schedules

### Beginner (New to role)
- **Daily**: 30-60 minutes
- **Weekly**: 5-7 hours
- **Focus**: Foundations and basics

### Intermediate (Some experience)
- **Daily**: 45-90 minutes
- **Weekly**: 7-10 hours
- **Focus**: Scenarios and practice

### Advanced (Experienced)
- **Daily**: 60-120 minutes
- **Weekly**: 10-15 hours
- **Focus**: Advanced topics and mentoring

---

## Effective Study Techniques

### 1. Active Recall
- Don't just read - practice explaining
- Test yourself regularly
- Use flashcards for key concepts

### 2. Spaced Repetition
- Review concepts multiple times
- Space out reviews (days, weeks)
- Focus on weak areas

### 3. Practice Scenarios
- Work through scenarios regularly
- Time yourself
- Get feedback

### 4. Teach Others
- Explain concepts to others
- Write summaries
- Create study guides

### 5. Reflect and Iterate
- Note what works
- Adjust your approach
- Focus on improvement

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Trying to learn everything at once**
- Focus on your role path
- Master foundations first
- Build incrementally

❌ **Only reading, not practicing**
- Practice scenarios regularly
- Explain concepts out loud
- Time yourself

❌ **Ignoring weak areas**
- Identify weak areas
- Focus on improvement
- Get feedback

❌ **Cramming before interview**
- Start early
- Build habits
- Practice regularly

❌ **Not asking for help**
- Join study groups
- Get feedback
- Learn from others
